Why compress images?

Large image files slow down websites, get rejected by upload forms, and clog inboxes. Compressing an image reduces its file size — making it faster to load, easier to share, and cheaper to store — with no visible difference in quality. Whether you're optimising images for a website, reducing photo size for WhatsApp, or preparing product photos for an online store, a few seconds in an image compressor saves kilobytes or even megabytes every time.

Where image compression matters most

Websites & SEO

Page load speed is a Google ranking factor. Compress and convert to WebP for the smallest files without quality loss. Faster pages rank better and reduce bounce rates.

WhatsApp & Social Media

WhatsApp auto-compresses shared photos aggressively. Pre-compressing to ~200 KB gives you control over the final look before platforms degrade your image further.

Email Attachments

Most email servers cap attachments at 25 MB. Compress product shots or event photos to a few hundred KB each so they clear the limit and load quickly for the recipient.

eCommerce & Online Stores

Product pages load faster with compressed images. Upload limits on Shopify, WooCommerce and similar platforms are easier to meet at smaller sizes, improving shopper experience.

What this compressor supports

Compress JPG / JPEG

A 3 MB photo can typically reach 300–500 KB at quality 80% with no visible degradation — ideal for web, email, and social sharing.

Compress PNG

Shrink screenshots, graphics, and logos. Use the resize or WebP output option for maximum size reduction while preserving transparency where needed.

Convert to WebP

WebP delivers superior compression. Converting a JPG to WebP at the same visual quality typically saves 25–35% more file size — the best format for modern websites.

Bulk Compression

Upload and compress multiple images in one go. Download them individually or as a single ZIP — no clicking through one at a time.

Target File Size (KB)

Need images under 200 KB for a form upload? Set a maximum KB limit and the compressor auto-adjusts quality to hit your target precisely.

Resize While Compressing

Set a target width or height in pixels. Resizing to your display dimensions is often the single biggest size reduction you can make.
