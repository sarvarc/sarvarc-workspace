PNG vs JPEG vs WebP: which should you use?

All three formats convert instantly with this tool — here's how to pick the right one for the job.

PNG

Lossless quality with full transparency support. Best for logos, screenshots, icons, and graphics with sharp edges or text. Files are larger than JPEG.

JPEG

Lossy compression built for photographs. Produces much smaller files than PNG but doesn't support transparency — transparent areas become white when converted.

WebP

A newer format combining PNG-level transparency with JPEG-level compression. Smaller than both for most images, and supported by all modern browsers.
